The Business Problem

CS teams find out customers are churning too late

In a SaaS business, by the time a customer formally cancels, the renewal conversation has already failed. The most effective Customer Success interventions happen 60–90 days before renewal — when there is still time to address adoption gaps, resolve support friction, and make a credible case for contract expansion or renewal.

The challenge is that churn risk is not uniformly distributed. Some accounts are deeply embedded — multi-year contracts, high feature adoption, strong NPS. Others are structurally fragile — month-to-month contracts, low tenure, minimal product usage. A CS team that treats all accounts equally will spend its time in the wrong places.

The Problem This Project Solves
"How do you identify which accounts need attention before they tell you — and prioritise CS effort where it will have the greatest revenue impact?"

This project builds a Customer Health Command Center: a live, interactive dashboard that scores every account on a composite health model, segments them by churn risk tier, surfaces the 90-day renewal pipeline, and provides an AI-powered recommendation engine for CS intervention strategy.

Data Engineering

Statistically generated from real distributions

Rather than hand-picking records or using a static sample, the 100 accounts in this dashboard are generated programmatically from the documented statistical distributions of the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset (Kaggle, n=7,043) — the most widely used public proxy for B2B SaaS churn data.

Why this matters
Hand-picked data produces hand-picked patterns. Generating accounts from real distributions means the dashboard reflects genuine analytical signal — the churn rates, contract mix, and tenure patterns that the Kaggle dataset actually documents — not the analyst's intuition about what the data should show.

The Model

A composite health scoring model

Each account receives a health score from 0–100, calculated as a weighted composite of five dimensions. The weights reflect the relative predictive power of each variable in the Kaggle churn dataset — contract type is the strongest single predictor of churn, followed by NPS as a direct sentiment signal.

Feature Adoption
Number of services/features adopted — the strongest long-term retention signal
30%
NPS Score
Direct customer sentiment — normalised from –100/+100 to 0–100
25%
Contract Type
Multi-year=100 · Annual=65 · Monthly=25 — contract structure is the #1 churn predictor
20%
Tenure
Months active, capped at 48 — longer tenure signals embedded value
15%
Support Burden
Inverted ticket volume — high open tickets reduce health score
10%

Accounts scoring below 35 are flagged as High Risk. Accounts scoring 35–54, or 55–64 with a renewal within 90 days, are flagged as Medium Risk. All others are Low Risk.

The Dashboard

Four screens, one command center

The dashboard is built as a fully interactive single-page application — no frameworks, no backend. Four screens cover the key workflows of a CS analyst: executive overview, account-level health, renewal pipeline, and AI-powered strategy recommendations.

SCREEN 01
Executive Overview
A summary view of the full account portfolio — key health metrics at a glance with four data visualisations showing how churn risk, contract type, tenure, and revenue exposure are distributed across accounts.
SCREEN 02
Account Health Table
A searchable, sortable table of all 100 accounts — each showing its health score, risk tier, contract type, and tenure. Filter by risk or contract type to zero in on the accounts that need attention.
SCREEN 03
90-Day Renewal Pipeline
A focused view of accounts renewing within the next 90 days, grouped by risk tier. Shows the revenue at stake per account so CS teams can prioritise outreach before renewal conversations begin.
SCREEN 04
AI Strategy Engine
A pre-built recommendation layer grounded in the portfolio data. Select a quick prompt, click an at-risk account, or type a custom query to surface CS strategy recommendations — covering prioritisation, renewal plays, onboarding improvements, and account-level action plans.
Key Findings

What the data surfaces

PRIMARY FINDING — CONTRACT TYPE IS THE DOMINANT CHURN DRIVER
Month-to-month customers churn at over 15× the rate of two-year contract holders
3× higher churn vs annual
Month-to-month accounts churn at 45.8% in the generated dataset vs. 0% for two-year contracts and 15.4% for annual contracts. This is the single most actionable finding: contract structure is not just a commercial preference — it is the strongest signal of long-term customer health. The most effective CS intervention is contract conversion, not reactive support.
FINDING 02 — EARLY TENURE IS THE HIGHEST-RISK WINDOW
The first 6 months carry disproportionate churn risk
0–6mo highest risk
Short-tenure accounts on month-to-month contracts have the steepest usage decline trends and lowest health scores. This is a time-to-value problem — customers who don't find product value quickly don't renew. Early onboarding intervention is the highest-ROI CS activity for this cohort.
FINDING 03 — SERVICE ADOPTION PREDICTS RETENTION
Customers using 3+ features cross a 90% retention threshold
2.4× better health score
Accounts with 5+ services adopted have 2.4× higher health scores than those using 1–2 features. Feature adoption is not just a product success metric — it is a leading indicator of renewal health. Product adoption campaigns have direct retention ROI.
Business Recommendations

From health scores to CS action

01
Early Intervention Program — trigger CS outreach at churn probability > 60%
High-risk accounts identified by the health scoring model should trigger a proactive CSM outreach sequence within 5 business days — before the account reaches a renewal decision in poor health. The dashboard's Renewal Pipeline screen surfaces exactly these accounts with ARR context.
Trigger: Health Score < 35
02
Onboarding Acceleration — "First 90 Days" program for new month-to-month accounts
New customers (tenure < 6 months) on monthly contracts with fewer than 2 features adopted represent the highest churn risk cohort. Automated in-product adoption nudges at Day 14, 30, and 60 — combined with a CSM touchpoint at Day 45 — would materially reduce early-stage churn.
Trigger: Tenure < 6mo + Features < 2
03
Product Adoption Campaign — "Get to 3" feature adoption drive
The 90% retention threshold at 3+ features makes product adoption campaigns directly revenue-relevant. Identifying accounts at 1–2 features with strong tenure and contract type — and running targeted feature discovery workshops — would improve health scores and reduce churn risk for the medium-risk cohort.
Trigger: Services < 3 + Health 35–60
04
Contract Upgrade Incentives — convert M-t-M to annual for accounts with tenure > 6 months
Month-to-month accounts with more than 6 months of tenure have demonstrated product stickiness but remain structurally fragile. A modest annual contract incentive — discounted rate, extended support, feature unlock — has significant ROI given that contract conversion alone moves churn probability from ~45% to ~11%.
Trigger: M-t-M + Tenure > 6mo + Health < 65
What This Demonstrates

The analytical and operational thinking

The most deliberate design decision in this project was generating accounts from documented statistical distributions rather than constructing a convenient sample. This distinction is analytically meaningful: when churn rates, contract mix, and tenure patterns are calibrated to the actual Kaggle data, the health scoring model reflects real B2B SaaS behaviour rather than assumed patterns.

The AI layer in Screen 4 was built to show how generative AI fits within — not instead of — structured analytical work. The recommendations are grounded in the specific dataset distributions, ensuring they are contextually relevant to the actual account data rather than generic responses to surface-level prompts.

"A health score is only as useful as the action it triggers. Every element of this dashboard — the risk tiers, the renewal pipeline, the AI engine — is designed around one question: what does a CS team need to see on Monday morning to know where to focus this week?"
